The horns of the giant sheep raised by Ostolaza shepherds are harvested as part of the minting of currency honored in the Estates, though their prevalence in Beville is greatly diminished by the greater emphasis on barter.
# Dimensions
The size of the horns is kept as consistent as possible through diet and breeding oversight by the Shepherds so that any horn can have the same quantities of each denomination “chipped” from it according to a regulated carpentry pattern.

Spiral-form horns occur on all but the rarest genetic exemplars of giant sheep, and some additionally have straight horns inboard of the spiraling pair. Spiral horns typically taper from a 5” cross-section at the base to 1” at the furthest usable point under the tip. The centerline approximation of such a horn is 72” long along 1.137 turns (409.3 degrees of arc), with one turn per 8”. This gives $C=\sqrt{(\frac{72}x)^2-8^2}\implies r=\frac C{2\pi}=10.$
